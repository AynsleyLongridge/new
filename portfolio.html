  • Everyday Magic : iShared

    Everyday Magic was a brief set by the IxDA student design challange. The aim was to focus on small interactions that you can improve in a massive way. Something that creates a simple, yet powerful moment.

    We can throw loose change into a charity box, but how long will it lie there for? We can sign up with an organisation to donate monthly, but how many of us are prepared to make that commitment? What if there was a simple, everyday magic solution to the problem of giving to charity?

    iShared is a one-tap-at-a-time donation station located in airports all over the globe, allowing you to instantly donate to support education worldwide. All with just one tap!

    Thanks to the magic of contactless payments, donations can be made effortlessly without the need to register your details. iShared gives you that, “I did something good!” feeling. Even better, it shows you the impact your gift has made by providing a fun and fact-filled receipt, showing you exactly how your gift has been used.



    Let’s make donating to charity more fun!

  • Elements website

    Using elements from the periodic table this project was created to help young children learn more about science in a fun and interesting way.

    My target audience for this brief was children aged 7-10. I kept my content low and tried to focus on making the learning experience more relatable and fun. I based my idea around five characters which represented different elements from the periodic table.

    For the illustrations in this project I used a graphics tablet and Illustrator to create drawings that were less structured for a more quirky approach.

    To make the website more interactive I included a quiz to allow the children to test themselves on how much they have learnt throughout the site.
